PennyHelm's niche is simple: it is open source and self-hostable like Actual Budget and Firefly III, but it also offers a polished, no-setup cloud option like YNAB and Monarch, at a lower price.

Replacing Mint

Intuit discontinued Mint, leaving many people looking for a new home for their bills, accounts, and net-worth tracking. PennyHelm covers that core workflow in one dashboard, and unlike Mint it gives you a free self-hosted option and full data ownership, plus a low-cost cloud tier if you would rather not run anything yourself.

If you want open source and self-hosting

Actual Budget and Firefly III are excellent open-source, self-hostable tools, and they are worth a look. PennyHelm differs mainly by offering an optional fully-hosted cloud (no server to maintain), a companion Android app, and built-in features like an interactive cashflow Sankey diagram, debt payoff planning with avalanche and snowball strategies, and tax-deduction tracking.

If you want a polished paid app

YNAB and Monarch Money are well-designed paid apps. PennyHelm aims to offer a similar hassle-free cloud experience at a lower price, while also giving you the option to self-host for free and keep your data entirely under your control. PennyHelm Cloud starts at $6.49/month billed annually, with a 30-day free trial and no credit card required.
